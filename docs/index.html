01 The gap

Slack has :shortcodes:. Discord has them. GitHub has them. But the apps you live in — iMessage, X, Mail, Notes, Safari, your IDE — don't.

macOS has a built-in picker at ⌃⌘Space. It's modal, slow, and breaks your flow. A tiny daemon that watches for :tada: and quietly swaps it for 🎉 — that's all you actually want.

02 Live suggestion popup

Type :sm —
pick with arrows.

A floating picker shows matches as you type. Arrow keys to navigate, Enter to accept, Escape to dismiss, or just keep typing to narrow down. Out of your way when you don't need it.

  • Fuzzy matching against 1,870 shortcodes
  • Anchors to the caret position, not the window
  • Keyboard-first — never steals mouse focus

03 What it does

Small surface.
Big reach.

Works in every app

Messages, X, Slack, VS Code, Mail, Safari, Notes, Terminal. If you can type in it, :shortcodes: work in it.

01

1,870 shortcodes

GitHub's gemoji set, bundled locally. :tada:, :fire:, :+1:, :rocket: — all the classics.

02

Atomic insertion

Even multi-codepoint emoji like 👨‍👩‍👧 (ZWJ) and 🇯🇵 (flag) insert as one glyph, never as fragments.

03

Password-safe

Detects secure-input fields and bypasses them automatically. Never touches your password typing.

04

Privacy-first

No clipboard. No network. No telemetry. Keystrokes pass through memory and are discarded. 100% offline.

05

Menu-bar only

No Dock icon. No ⌘Tab clutter. A small icon that stays out of your way until you need it.
